Laing

Laing is a flavorful Bicolano dish made from dried taro leaves cooked in coconut milk and infused with the heat of chili peppers. It’s often paired with rice and can be cooked with pork or shrimp.

Ingredients:

  • 200g dried taro leaves (you can also use fresh if available)
  • 1 can (400ml) coconut milk
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1 onion (chopped)
  • 4 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 2-3 long green chili peppers (sliced)
  • 1-2 red chili peppers (sliced, for heat)
  • 200g pork belly or shrimp (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon shrimp paste (bagoong)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil

Instructions:

  1. In a pot, heat the oil and sauté onions, garlic, and chili peppers until softened.
  2. Add the pork belly (if using) and cook until browned. If using shrimp, add it later to avoid overcooking.
  3. Stir in the shrimp paste and cook for 1-2 minutes.
  4. Add the dried taro leaves and mix well.
  5. Pour in the coconut milk and water, then bring it to a simmer.
  6. Cover and cook for 20-30 minutes until the taro leaves are tender.
  7. Season with salt and pepper. If using shrimp, add it near the end of cooking.
  8. Serve hot with steamed rice.
