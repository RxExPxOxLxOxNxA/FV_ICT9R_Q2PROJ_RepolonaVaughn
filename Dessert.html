Suman sa Lihia

Suman sa Lihia is a traditional Bicolano dessert made from glutinous rice cooked with lye water and coconut milk, wrapped in banana leaves. It has a unique savory-sweet flavor, perfect for a sweet ending to any meal.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups glutinous rice
  • 1 1/2 cups water
  • 1 tablespoon lye water (Lihiya)
  • Banana leaves (for wrapping, cut into 6-inch squares)
  • 1/2 cup sugar (for serving)
  • Grated coconut (optional, for serving)

Instructions:

  1. Wash the glutinous rice thoroughly and soak it in water for 4-6 hours or overnight.
  2. In a pot, boil the rice with 1 1/2 cups of water and lye water. Stir occasionally to prevent it from sticking. Cook until the rice is soft and absorbed all the water.
  3. Once the rice is cooked, allow it to cool slightly.
  4. Place a small amount of the cooked rice onto the center of a banana leaf square. Fold the leaf into a tight package, folding in the sides and then rolling it up like a cylinder.
  5. Steam the wrapped rice for 30-40 minutes.
  6. Serve with sugar and grated coconut on top.
